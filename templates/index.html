What is Diabetic Retinopathy?

A diabetes complication that affects eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye (retina).

Diabetic Retinopathy Symptoms

Here are some Diabetic Retinopathy Symptoms that could be found in people with the infected eyes.

  • Have blurred vision
  • Suffer from the impairment of color vision
  • Seeing an increasing number of floaters
  • Patches or streaks that block the vision
  • Have poor night vision
  • Sudden and total loss of vision

It is important to make sure that the risk of vision loss is minimized. The only way people with diabetes can prevent DR is to attend every eye examination scheduled by their doctor.

Complications

Some complications that can be caused by diabetic retinopathy

Vitreous Hemorrhage

The new blood vessels may bleed into the clear, jelly-like substance that fills the center of the eye. If the amount of bleeding is small, only a few dark spots (floaters) can be seen

Retinal Detachment

The abnormal blood vessels associated with diabetic retinopathy stimulate the growth of scar tissue, which can pull the retina away from the back of the eye

Glaucoma

New blood vessels may grow in the front part of the eye and interfere with the normal flow of fluid out of the eye, causing pressure in the eye to build up.

Blindness

Eventually, diabetic retinopathy, glaucoma or both can lead to complete vision loss.

Diabetic Retinopathy Severity

The severity level of diabetic retinopathy can be divided into 5 classes

No Diabetic Retinopathy

Level 0

Healthy eyes without any symptoms of diabetic retinopathy.

Mild

Level 1

At this stage, microaneurysms occur. They are small areas of balloon-like swelling in the retina's tiny blood vessels.

Moderate

Level 2

This stage is when blood vessels that nourish the retina are blocked.

Severe

Level 3

In this stage, many more blood vessels are blocked, depriving several areas of the retina with their blood supply. These areas of the retina send signals to the body to grow new blood vessels for nourishment.

Proliferate

Level 4

At this stage, the signals sent by the retina for nourishment trigger the growth of new blood vessels. By themselves, these blood vessels do not cause symptoms or vision loss. However, if they leak blood, severe vision loss and blindness can result.
